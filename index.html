⚡ Tip of the Month

The "Role + Task + Format" Formula

The single most effective way to get better AI results — every time.

Try this structure: "You are a [role]. [Task description]. Output as [format]."

Example: "You are a senior HR manager. Write a professional email declining a job applicant. Keep it warm, brief, and under 100 words."

This simple formula works for almost any task and consistently produces more useful, on-target results than vague requests.

💼 Real Work Use Case

Finance Team Cuts Report Time by 60%

How one team used AI to transform their monthly reporting process.

The challenge: The finance team spent 3 hours every month manually formatting and writing the management summary for their financial reports.

The solution: They paste their raw numbers into ChatGPT with a simple prompt: "Summarize these figures for a non-finance executive audience. Highlight key trends and flag anything unusual."

The result: First draft in 5 minutes. Total time saved: ~2.5 hours per month.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Treating AI Output as Final

The most common mistake beginners make — and how to avoid it.

The mistake: Copying AI output directly without reviewing it. AI can be confidently wrong — it doesn't know your company, your context, or the latest facts.

The fix: Always treat AI output as a first draft. Read it. Edit it. Check any facts it mentions. Add your own judgment before sending or using it.

Remember: AI is a powerful assistant, not a replacement for your expertise and accountability.

The 5-Minute Email Makeover

A prompt you can use right now to improve any email you've written.

Copy this prompt:

"Review this email and improve it. Make it clearer, more professional, and more concise. Keep the same meaning but remove any unnecessary words. Here is the email: [paste your email]"

Why it works: This is one of the highest-value uses of AI for everyday work. You keep control of the message; AI just makes it better.

📋 Guidelines

AI Dos & Don'ts

A quick reference to help you use AI safely, responsibly, and effectively at work.

✅ Do this

  • ✓Always review AI output before using it. Treat it as a smart first draft, not a finished product.
  • ✓Be specific in your prompts. The more context you give, the better the result. Include role, task, and format.
  • ✓Use AI for repetitive writing tasks like emails, summaries, and templates — this is where it saves the most time.
  • ✓Fact-check important claims. AI can be confidently wrong. Verify anything critical before sharing.
  • ✓Iterate and refine. If the first result isn't right, ask AI to adjust it. Say "make it shorter" or "make it more formal."
  • ✓Use AI to learn. Ask it to explain things you don't understand — it's a patient, always-available teacher.
  • ✓Keep a prompt library. When you find a prompt that works well, save it for reuse.

❌ Don't do this

  • ✕Never share confidential data with public AI tools. No client names, financial figures, or internal strategies.
  • ✕Don't submit AI output without reading it. You are responsible for everything you send or publish.
  • ✕Don't assume AI is always right. It can hallucinate facts, dates, and names with complete confidence.
  • ✕Avoid vague prompts. "Write something about sales" will get you a generic result. Be specific about what you need.
  • ✕Don't use AI to replace your judgment. Use it to support your thinking, not substitute it.
  • ✕Don't share AI-generated content as your own original research or expertise without disclosure.
  • ✕Don't give up after one bad result. Rephrase your prompt, add more context, and try again.
