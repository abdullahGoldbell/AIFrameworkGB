### **Prompt 10 — Priority Medical Supply Transport**

Backstory:

 You manage emergency logistics for a hospital network in Singapore (covering SGH, NUH, TTSH, KTPH, and CGH) that frequently needs to move critical medical supplies such as blood units, oxygen cylinders, and transplant organs between facilities. Delays can cost lives, but the routes often cross congested zones along the CTE, PIE, and CBD during peak hours. Current dispatch methods do not differentiate between critical and non-critical medical items, causing slower response times. You want a priority-based transport system that guarantees fastest delivery for life-saving cargo.

Goal:

 Design an emergency medical transport routing plan that guarantees minimum delivery times for critical supplies.

Prompt:

 "You are an Emergency Medical Logistics Planner. Develop a routing system for critical medical supplies across Singapore’s public hospital network:

  - Categorise deliveries based on urgency and medical priority.

  - Create fast-track routes with SPF Traffic Police coordination where applicable.

  - Assign vehicles based on speed and load suitability.

  - Integrate live LTA traffic data for real-time rerouting.

  - Include post-delivery reports for MOH compliance and quality checks."

Inputs Required:

  - List of hospitals & coordinates

  - Item urgency classification

  - Vehicle types and speeds

  - Traffic pattern data

  - Delivery time goals for each urgency level
