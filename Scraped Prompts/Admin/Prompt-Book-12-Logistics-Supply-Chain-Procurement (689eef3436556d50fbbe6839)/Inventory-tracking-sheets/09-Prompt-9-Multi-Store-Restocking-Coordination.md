### **Prompt 9 — Multi-Store Restocking Coordination**

Backstory:

 A retail apparel chain has 25 outlets across Singapore—spread across Orchard, Marina Bay, Jurong, Tampines, and major suburban malls—each with different sales velocities. Some stores overstock while others run out, leading to uneven distribution and unnecessary procurement costs. You want a system to coordinate restocking by shifting inventory between stores before placing new supplier orders.

Goal:

 Create an inter-store transfer-based restocking system.

Prompt:

 "You are a Retail Supply Chain Planner. Design a restocking plan for a multi-store apparel chain.

  - Track weekly sales velocity for each store.

  - Identify surplus stock locations and shortage locations.

  - Recommend stock transfers between stores before supplier ordering.

  - Calculate cost savings from reduced procurement.

  - Provide a monthly store balance scorecard."

Inputs Required:

  - Store list with stock levels

  - Sales velocity per SKU per store

  - Transfer logistics costs

  - Supplier lead times

  - Restocking threshold rules
