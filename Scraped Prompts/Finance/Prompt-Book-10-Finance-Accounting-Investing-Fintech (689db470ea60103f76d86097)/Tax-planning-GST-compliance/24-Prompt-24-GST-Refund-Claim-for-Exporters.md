### **Prompt 24 — GST Refund Claim for Exporters**

Backstory:

 A 28-year-old lifestyle goods exporter based in Singapore ships products to Europe and the US. She incurs GST on local raw material purchases but exports finished goods as zero-rated supplies under IRAS rules. She wants to claim a refund of the accumulated input tax quickly and without errors, and is also evaluating whether the Major Exporter Scheme (MES) would suit her cash-flow profile.

Goal:

 Guide through GST refund claims and zero-rating compliance for Singapore exporters.

Prompt:

 "You are a Singapore GST export compliance advisor. Create a step-by-step guide to claim a refund of net input tax on zero-rated export supplies through the GST F5.

 Include:

  - Preparing required documentation (export evidence, bills of lading / airway bills, customs permits)

  - Filing the GST F5 with refund claim via myTax Portal and the IRAS InvoiceNow / e-records expectations

  - Common mistakes to avoid (insufficient export evidence, wrong supply classification)

  - Whether to apply for the Major Exporter Scheme (MES) for upfront GST suspension"

Inputs Required:

  - Purchase tax invoices with GST paid

  - Export invoices, customs export permits, and shipping documents

  - Bank inward remittance advices
