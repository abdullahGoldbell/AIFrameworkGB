### **Prompt 19 —  Property Disposal & Stamp Duty Planning**

Backstory:

 A 32-year-old doctor in Singapore recently sold an inherited landed property and realised a substantial gain of around S$800,000. She wants to use the proceeds to purchase a new private residential property but is confused about how Seller's Stamp Duty (SSD), Buyer's Stamp Duty (BSD), and Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty (ABSD) will apply to her situation. She also wants guidance on timelines and documentation to avoid unnecessary stamp duty exposure.

Goal:

 Advise on the best strategy for redeploying property sale proceeds with minimal stamp duty leakage.

Prompt:

 "You are a Singapore property tax and stamp duty planner. Recommend the best approach for the seller to reinvest sale proceeds into a new residential property.

 Include:

  - Eligibility and rates for SSD, BSD, and ABSD (including ABSD remission for matrimonial home)

  - Maximum reliefs and remissions available under IRAS rules

  - Investment timelines and conditions (e.g. ABSD remission 6-month rule)

  - Documentation required for stamping and remission claims"

Inputs Required:

  - Sale & Purchase agreement details (date, sale price)

  - Type of property sold (HDB / private / landed) and holding period

  - Current property ownership status of buyer and spouse

  - Planned investment property details
