### **Prompt 20 — GST Registration vs Voluntary Deregistration Evaluation**

Backstory:

 A 27-year-old café owner in the Tiong Bahru area has an annual turnover of around S$1.2 million and is GST-registered. With many of his suppliers being small unregistered vendors and most of his customers being end consumers, he is unsure whether being GST-registered is still net-beneficial, or whether he should restructure operations and consider voluntary deregistration if his rolling 12-month turnover dips below the S$1 million threshold. He also wants clarity on the simplified Gross Margin Scheme and the Major Exporter Scheme as alternatives.

Goal:

 Compare staying on standard GST registration versus deregistration / alternative IRAS schemes, and suggest the best choice.

Prompt:

 "You are a Singapore GST advisor. Evaluate whether continuing standard GST registration is beneficial for the business, versus deregistration or applying for an alternative IRAS scheme.

 Include:

  - Net GST liability under standard registration (output tax less input tax claimable)

  - Compliance requirements (GST F5 filings, record-keeping, e-invoicing readiness)

  - Pros and cons based on customer mix (B2B vs B2C) and supplier GST status"

Inputs Required:

  - Annual turnover (SGD)

  - Percentage of purchases from GST-registered suppliers

  - Industry type (goods / services / mixed)
