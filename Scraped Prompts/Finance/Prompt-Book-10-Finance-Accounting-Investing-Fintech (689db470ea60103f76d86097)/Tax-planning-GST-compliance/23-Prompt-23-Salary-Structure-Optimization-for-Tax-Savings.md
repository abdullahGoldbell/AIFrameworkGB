### **Prompt 23 — Salary Structure Optimisation for Tax Savings**

Backstory:

 A 30-year-old sales manager working in Singapore receives a fixed annual package but can request changes to remuneration components during his annual review. He wants to restructure his package to reduce taxable employment income — possibly by adjusting fixed vs variable mix, opting into the SRS (Supplementary Retirement Scheme), making voluntary CPF top-ups, and structuring eligible reimbursements (e.g. transport, mobile, professional development) — without reducing in-hand pay.

Goal:

 Design a tax-optimised salary structure under Singapore tax rules.

Prompt:

 "You are a Singapore payroll tax planner. Suggest changes to remuneration components to reduce taxable income without lowering net take-home pay.

 Include:

  - Allowances and reimbursements with favourable IRAS treatment

  - Use of SRS contributions, voluntary CPF top-ups (CPF Cash Top-Up Relief) and other personal reliefs

  - Revised gross and assessable income comparison (before vs after restructure), respecting the S$80,000 Personal Income Tax Relief Cap

  - Justification for each change with the relevant IRAS guidance"

Inputs Required:

  - Current package structure with all components

  - Singapore tax residency status and citizenship / PR / EP

  - Eligible reimbursements and benefits-in-kind under employer policy
