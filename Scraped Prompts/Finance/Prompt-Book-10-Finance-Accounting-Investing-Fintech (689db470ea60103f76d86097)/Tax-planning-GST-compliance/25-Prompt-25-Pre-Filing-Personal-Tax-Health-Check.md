### **Prompt 25 — Pre-Filing Personal Tax Health Check**

Backstory:

 A 33-year-old HR manager in Singapore files her personal income tax return with IRAS every April but often scrambles last minute to verify her auto-included income, donations, and reliefs, leading to missed deductions. She wants a "tax health check" three months before the filing deadline so she can prepare everything in advance and review her draft assessment in myTax Portal calmly.

Goal:

 Create a pre-filing personal tax readiness checklist for Singapore taxpayers.

Prompt:

 "You are a Singapore personal tax consultant. Prepare a pre-filing tax readiness checklist for salaried individuals filing through myTax Portal.

 Include:

  - Verifying auto-included employment income (Auto-Inclusion Scheme — AIS), CPF contributions, and donations to IPCs

  - Reviewing supporting documents for personal reliefs (Earned Income Relief, Spouse / Parent Relief, NSman, Course Fees, CPF Cash Top-Up, SRS)

  - Listing rental income, dividend, and trade/freelance income with supporting records

  - Cross-checking against the S$80,000 Personal Income Tax Relief Cap and choosing the most efficient relief mix"

Inputs Required:

  - Salary structure and IR8A / employer AIS submission summary

  - List of reliefs to claim

  - Investment, insurance, SRS, and CPF top-up records
