### **Prompt 16 — Tax Planning for First-Time Home Buyer**

Backstory:

 A 30-year-old banking professional in Singapore has just booked her first private condominium unit worth S$1.2 million in the Tampines area, with a bank loan sanctioned for 75% of the value (within MAS LTV limits). She is aware that there are reliefs and grants available — including the use of CPF Ordinary Account funds for downpayment and monthly instalments, the Buyer's Stamp Duty (BSD) structure, and the property tax owner-occupier rates — but she's unsure how to optimise these in the first year since her monthly instalments start mid-year. She wants a clear, optimised plan to ensure no eligible benefit is missed.

Goal:

 Prepare a year-wise tax and CPF benefit plan for a first-time home buyer.

Prompt:

 "You are a Singapore housing loan and tax specialist. For the given home loan details, prepare a plan to maximise reliefs and CPF utilisation in the current and following years.

 Include:

  - Use of CPF Ordinary Account for downpayment and monthly instalments

  - Buyer's Stamp Duty (BSD) and Additional Buyer's Stamp Duty (ABSD) treatment for first-time buyers

  - Property tax owner-occupier concessionary rates and application timing

  - Tips to align loan disbursement, CPF withdrawal limits, and key occupation date for better cash-flow and benefits"

Inputs Required:

  - Loan offer letter with disbursement / instalment start date

  - Loan amount, tenure, and interest rate

  - Date of TOP / key collection / SLA registration

  - Annual income and CPF contribution status
